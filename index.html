About Magnolias

Magnolia is the name of a genus of plant (mostly trees and bushes) with about 210 subspecies in its group. It is an ancient genus, predating bees. It is said that the flowers evolved to encourage pollination from beetles, and the flowers are especially tough to protect them from harm. When people are commonly referring to Magnolias, they are most likely talking about the most prevalant species, the Southern Magnolia, but there are many others.

Magnolias are very integral to southern culture in the United States, since they are so abundant in Southeastern USA. It is not uncommon for southern women to be compared to Magnolias since they are exceptionally beautiful and strong.

The USDA has created a heartiness chart based off of weather and climate to assign a number system to certain areas. This way we can more accurately identify if a plant is best suited to grow in a certain part of the country. All of the plants listed have their USDA Zone range in the caption so that you can know which Magnolia would be right for you.
